Rainfall and temperature impact whether flora and fauna can flourish, may impede human recreation opportunities both in the summer and winter, as well as pose threats to the agricultural industry of the catchment. {% endcall %} {% call paragraph.render() %} The range of some invasive species may increase within the Upper Clutha Catchment: for example, the sub-tropical bungalow palm, which currently only grows on the North Island, could spread to the South Island under a warming climate2.
